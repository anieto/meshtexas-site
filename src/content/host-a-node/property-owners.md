MeshTexas is looking for property owners willing to host a small radio repeater — the fixed-location devices that extend coverage across the mesh network. Every repeater relays traffic for the whole network, so a well-placed one makes a real difference for everyone nearby.

**What we're asking for:** space for a small radio and antenna, ideally somewhere elevated — a roof, a tall structure, anywhere with a clear view of the surrounding area helps radio range the most.

**What's involved:**
- No cost to you, and no offer from us either — this is a simple, no-strings request for space.
- Installation and any maintenance is handled by MeshTexas volunteers, scheduled with you in advance.
- Access is entirely **revocable** — if you ever want the equipment removed, just let us know and we will.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the node used for?**

It extends MeshTexas — a free, open-source community mesh network that lets people send text messages over long-range radio, without cell service or internet. It's especially useful for emergency readiness and staying in touch when normal infrastructure is down.

**What equipment gets installed?**

A small, weatherproof enclosure containing the radio and battery, a lightweight antenna, and usually a solar panel. Compact — roughly the size of a home router, with a longer antenna.

**How is it powered?**

Solar, and self-sufficient — no connection to your building's electrical system.

**What does mounting involve?**

Usually just the antenna, sometimes on a short mast or pole nearby. No cabling runs into the building. Mounting is done with magnets, U-bolts, or heavy-duty zip ties — no drilling unless we agree to it in advance.

**How much maintenance does it need?**

Very little. Occasional visits — maybe once or twice a year — for firmware updates or a hardware check. We'll agree on access terms in writing.

**What range does it support?**

Depends on elevation and terrain, but typically a few miles per hop, more from higher ground. Devices connect across longer distances through automatic mesh relaying. Operates in the license-free 915 MHz ISM band.

**Will it interfere with anything on my property?**

Very unlikely — this operates on different frequencies than Wi-Fi, cellular, or TV, at very low power, and is FCC-compliant. Happy to provide documentation if you'd like it.

**What about liability?**

The property owner typically carries liability, though the likelihood of any incident is extremely remote.

**Any permits, HOA, or zoning concerns?**

Small, non-commercial antenna installs generally don't need permits. HOA or building rules may restrict permanent changes — that's exactly why nothing here is permanent or requires drilling. If you're a tenant or on shared property, written permission from the owner is a good idea.

**Does this affect lightning risk?**

No — solar power is actually one reason we avoid tying the node into your building's electrical system at all.

**How long does it stay, and can it be removed?**

As long as you'd like. No long-term obligation — you can request removal at any time, with reasonable notice to coordinate, and it leaves no permanent marks.
